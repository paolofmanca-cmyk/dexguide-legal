1. Who We Are

DexGuide is an independent iOS companion app for classic Pokémon games (Generations II–IV). It is developed and maintained as an indie project. DexGuide is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to Nintendo, Game Freak, or The Pokémon Company.

This policy explains what data we collect when you use DexGuide, how it is used, and your rights regarding that data. When this policy says "we", "us", or "our", it refers to the developer(s) of DexGuide.

2. Data We Do NOT Collect

DexGuide is designed to work entirely without an account. We collect no personally identifiable information.

Specifically, we do not collect or store:

  • Your name, email address, or any other contact information
  • Your Apple ID or any account credentials
  • Your precise or approximate location
  • Your IP address (we do not operate any servers that receive it)
  • Photos, camera, or microphone data
  • Contacts or calendar data
  • Health or fitness data
  • Any data about other apps on your device
  • Browsing history or search history outside the app

3. Data Stored Locally on Your Device

All user-generated content is stored locally on your device using iOS standard storage (UserDefaults, Core Data). This includes:

  • Your selected game and app preferences
  • Pokémon caught / seen status (catch checklist)
  • Saved teams and team folders
  • Shiny hunt session logs and encounter counts
  • Nuzlocke run data (encounters, roster, death log)
  • Story chapter progress and item checklists

This data never leaves your device unless you are a Pro subscriber with iCloud sync enabled (see Section 5).

4. Analytics & Purchase Management — Third-Party SDKs

DexGuide uses two third-party SDKs that receive limited, anonymous data. Neither receives any personally identifiable information.

Amplitude (Usage Analytics)

We use Amplitude to collect anonymous, aggregated usage analytics. This helps us understand which features are useful and where the app can be improved.

What Amplitude receives:

  • Anonymous event names (e.g. "Pokédex tab opened", "shiny hunt started")
  • The game you have selected (e.g. "Emerald") — stored as a user property, not linked to any identity
  • App version and iOS version (sent by the SDK automatically)
  • Session duration and event counts

What Amplitude does NOT receive:

  • Any personally identifiable information
  • Your name, email, or Apple ID
  • Your precise device identifier (IDFA/IDFV is not collected)
  • Your location

Amplitude is configured with its EU data residency zone, meaning event data is stored in EU-based infrastructure. Amplitude's privacy policy is available at amplitude.com/privacy.

RevenueCat (Subscription & Purchase Management)

We use RevenueCat to manage in-app purchase validation, subscription status, and entitlements. RevenueCat receives your App Store transaction receipts and a randomly generated anonymous user ID in order to verify and restore purchases. It does not receive your name, email address, or Apple ID.

What RevenueCat receives:

  • A randomly generated anonymous user ID (not linked to any personal identity)
  • App Store purchase receipts (used solely to verify subscription status)
  • App version and iOS version

What RevenueCat does NOT receive:

  • Your name, email, or Apple ID
  • Any payment or billing information
  • Your location or device identifier

RevenueCat's privacy policy is available at revenuecat.com/privacy.

We do not sell or share any data received via these SDKs with third parties for advertising purposes.

5. iCloud Sync (Pro Subscribers Only)

If you subscribe to DexGuide Pro, your user data (teams, shiny hunts, Nuzlocke runs, caught status) is synced via Apple iCloud (CloudKit and NSUbiquitousKeyValueStore). This sync is handled entirely by Apple — the data is stored in your personal iCloud account, which we have no access to.

Apple's iCloud privacy terms are available at apple.com/legal/privacy.

iCloud sync can be disabled at any time via your iOS Settings → [Your name] → iCloud → DexGuide.

6. In-App Purchases

DexGuide Pro subscriptions and lifetime purchases are processed entirely by Apple's App Store via StoreKit 2. We do not receive, store, or process your payment information. Purchase validation and entitlement management is handled by RevenueCat (see Section 4). We only receive a confirmation of whether a valid purchase exists — never your billing details.

Apple's App Store privacy policy governs the payment transaction: apple.com/legal/privacy.

7. Advertising

DexGuide contains no advertising of any kind. We do not integrate any advertising SDK. We do not use your data to serve personalised or contextual ads, and we do not share any data with advertising networks.

8. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, trade, or share your personal data with third parties. The only third-party services that receive any data from the app are Amplitude and RevenueCat (both described in Section 4), and the data they receive is anonymous.

9. Children's Privacy

DexGuide is rated 4+ on the App Store and is suitable for all ages. We do not knowingly collect personal information from any user, including children under 13 (or the applicable age in your jurisdiction). Because we collect no personal data at all, DexGuide complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and equivalent regulations by design.

10. Data Retention and Deletion

Because all user data is stored locally on your device (or in your personal iCloud account), you are in full control:

  • Delete app data: Delete the app from your device. All local data is removed by iOS automatically.
  • Delete iCloud data: Go to Settings → [Your name] → iCloud → Manage Storage → DexGuide → Delete Data.
  • Anonymous analytics (Amplitude): Amplitude retains anonymised event data for up to 24 months. Because the data is not linked to any identity, it cannot be individually deleted. You may contact us and we will submit a deletion request to Amplitude on your behalf where technically feasible.
  • Purchase records (RevenueCat): RevenueCat retains anonymous purchase records as required to validate and restore purchases. These are not linked to your personal identity. Contact us if you wish to request deletion.

11. Your Rights (GDPR / UK GDPR / CCPA)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, California, or another jurisdiction with data protection laws, you may have the following rights. Because DexGuide collects no personal data, most of these rights apply in a limited way — but we are happy to assist:

  • Right to access: Request confirmation of what data (if any) we hold about you.
  • Right to erasure: Request deletion of any data associated with you.
  • Right to object: Object to any processing of your data.
  • Right to portability: Request your data in a portable format.
  • Right to lodge a complaint: Contact your local data protection authority if you have concerns.

California residents: DexGuide does not sell personal information and does not share it for cross-context behavioural advertising. No opt-out is required, but you may contact us if you have questions.

12. App Store Tracking Transparency

DexGuide does not use the App Tracking Transparency (ATT) framework and does not request permission to track you across other companies' apps or websites. We do not track you.

13. Changes to This Policy

If we make material changes to this privacy policy, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. For significant changes, we will include a notice within the app. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

Continued use of DexGuide after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.
